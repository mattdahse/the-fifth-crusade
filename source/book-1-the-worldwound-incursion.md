# Book I — The Worldwound Incursion

*The Fifth Crusade — Book I*

## **The Gathering in the Dark**

<!-- fathom: 165703544 -->

*October 17, 2024 session — the caverns beneath **Kenabres**, in the first hours after the demon-fall*

![Strangers in the ruin beneath **Kenabres** — a guttering holy symbol, a hand out of the rubble, and a darkness that pressed upon the eyes like a hand.](images/waking-among-the-ruins.png)

When the streets of **Kenabres** broke open and swallowed the festival crowds whole, those who lived woke to a darkness so complete it pressed upon the eyes like a hand. Dust choked the air. Somewhere far off a wounded dog yelped beneath the rubble until, at last, it did not. And in that black ruin, strangers who had never spoken began to call out to one another, and by their voices alone became a company.

### **Waking Among the Ruins**

**Lupenor Celest**, the elf, came to her senses upon a shifting slope of shattered cobblestone and splintered beam. Somewhere above her a woman's ragged breathing told of one pinned and crushed, and though she could see nothing, she crawled toward the sound through the treacherous dark. Not far off, **Varic Sarian**, priest of **Sarenrae**, dragged a whole sentence past the grit in his throat and set light upon his holy symbol — a glow the thick dust drank almost to nothing. By that muted radiance he found a man's hand gripping his ankle: **Horgus Gwerm**, a nobleman in ruined finery, who recoiled from help even as he demanded it.

Elsewhere the young sorcerer **Rabiah** kindled a light of her own and clawed away the wreckage of a vendor's cart to uncover a blind elf, his face torn by some demonic lash, his eyes destroyed. This was **Aravashnial**, and he gripped her sleeve and asked only whether any others yet lived.

### **The Freeing of Anevia**

![Found rope, a splintered beam, and **Harlock's** back against the stone — no magic and no glory, only four strangers and a rock.](images/the-freeing-of-anevia.png)

The pinned woman was **Anevia**, one leg broken beneath a great block of stone. With a length of found rope and the strength of **Harlock Greyforge** — the stalwart of **Iomedae**, who had come to the festival of **Armasse** to swear his oath and be raised a paladin, and whose vigil the demons had broken before ever the words could leave him — the company rolled the stone aside and drew her free. **Varic** knit the worst of her wounds with the healing grace of his goddess, splinted the shattered limb, and fashioned a crutch. For **Aravashnial** he could do less; the ruin of the elf's eyes lay beyond any power he held, and he could offer only to staunch the bleeding and speak the plain, hard truth. The elf accepted it with the weary dignity of one who had expected no better.

Even here, gathered and grieving, they were not of one mind. **Horgus** dismissed the lot of them as amateurs and set himself to giving orders; **Aravashnial**, who claimed to have fought demons before, was cowed into silence by the noble's scorn.

### **The Spawn of the Dead Spider**

Scouting the cavern's edge, **Rabiah** found the great upturned corpse of a black widow — and from a wound in its bloated abdomen slithered pale, undulating maggots the size of a man's arm, groping toward her. She loosed a bolt; **Varic** hurled a lance of fire; and **Harlock**, drawing his sword, clove one of the vile things in twain so that its digested filth pooled steaming between the halves. The second was pinned and finished at dagger's point. It was no great battle, but it was their first shed together.

![Four silvered scales of **Terendelev**, cold and lovely in the dark — and **Harlock**, alone among them, looking up.](images/the-scales-of-terendelev.png)

Among the debris they uncovered four silvered scales, each the span of a splayed hand and glowing softly — fragments, it was said, of the fallen dragon **Terendelev**. As each of the four laid hand upon one, its gift made itself known: to one the buoyancy of rolling cloud, to another the veil of a false face, to another a ward against harm. **Harlock**, receiving his, looked up toward the unseen vault of the cave and clutched at a pendant beneath his mail, and said nothing at all.

### **The Roads Below**

They ordered their march — **Harlock** at the fore, the blind elf and the crippled woman sheltered in the middle, **Lupenor** guarding the rear — and pressed ever upward toward the surface they could not yet reach. As they climbed the uneven ground, **Horgus** let it be known that their labor would not go unpaid: any who stayed at his side to the end, and saw him back to his estate, would share a thousand gold pieces between them, with further reward besides. The company allowed that they would do their best to keep him alive. A masterwork pack was recovered from a high ledge, scaled by **Harlock** as nimbly as a spider; within lay arrows, caltrops, and healing draughts. Deeper still, a cave viper the length of a tall man burst from the fallen rock and struck at **Harlock**, who turned it on his shield until sword and crossbow-bolt laid it low.

### **The Temple of Torag**

At last the passages opened upon a windowless bunker of worked stone, its facade graven with a great hammer — a shrine of **Torag**, the Father of the Forge, long abandoned in the deep. A giant fly clung to its wall; as they closed on it, **Varic** laid a hand upon **Lupenor** and called down a fleeting favor of his goddess to steady the elf's aim, and the thing was soon crushed beneath **Harlock's** blade. Within the antechamber a basin of water stood still sanctified, holy water yet, though the ground about it had lost its consecration — not defiled, **Varic** judged, only no longer hallowed as it once had been; he filled his vials from it. In the sanctuary beyond, a dust-shrouded dwarf sat facing the altar — and rose with a shriek, plainly some undead thing, hostile at once. It flung itself at the company and was destroyed by a single, telling stroke of **Harlock's** sword.

### **A Safe Haven Restored**

![The shrine of **Torag** made holy again by their own hands — and **Harlock** playing a low, sad tune into the dark while the others sleep.](images/a-safe-haven-restored.png)

**Horgus**, in his fear, slipped away into the dark, and the company chose — against grievance and good sense both — to go after him. **Varic's** patient words turned the noble back, and together they returned to the shrine. For eight long hours they labored to cleanse and reconsecrate the temple of **Torag**, and when it was done a quiet sense of safety settled over them, a feeling of being watched over by a kindly god. **Varic** spent the last of his gathered grace to heal the wounded. Weary and far from their goal, they knew well how much road still lay between them and the surface.

In the hush that followed, tongues loosened. **Anevia** wept at last for the wife she had left above, a paladin even now battling demons in the streets, and took comfort in **Varic's** kindness — and warmed toward the company for having risked themselves to fetch back the very man who had scorned her. **Aravashnial** asked each in turn their calling, taking the measure of the strangers into whose keeping he had fallen. Watches were set. The deep cold pressed in, and **Varic**, seeing the shivering nobleman without so much as a bedroll, gave over his own blanket freely; **Horgus** pressed a platinum piece upon him in return. **Harlock** drew out a set of pan pipes and played a sad, low tune into the dark.

So the first night beneath **Kenabres** passed without further terror, in a house made holy again by their own hands.

## **Beneath the Wounded City**

<!-- fathom: 178009368 -->

*November 8, 2024 session — the lightless tunnels beneath Kenabres, in the days after the city's fall*

### **A Watch in the Reconsecrated Dark**

Eight hours of labor had gone into the small forge-temple before the company laid down to sleep — scrubbing away the filth of the undead priest who had defiled it, and consecrating the altar anew to the old craftsman-god of the deep. It was honest, exhausting work, and it left them a rare feeling of safety in the belly of the earth. Yet the ground would not be still. Through the night the cavern trembled with aftershocks, and silt sifted from the ceiling while dust settled in the distance with the sound of falling stone. Something vast was happening far above them, in the ruin of **Kenabres**, and its echoes reached even here.

The watch passed without incident. Come the reckoned morning, **Varic Sarian** conjured clean water so that the weary and the wounded might wash the blood and grime from their skins, and offered up prayers of thanks to **Sarenrae** for another dawn survived. **Harlock Greyforge** took himself outside to heave stones overhead in his morning discipline, then shared out his ample rations so that none of their charges went hungry. When he brought Varic the masterwork warhammer he had won from the dead priest, the priest took it in both hands and asked the Dawnflower to make it fly straight and true against their foes. **Lupenor Celest** pressed a handful of her own arrows upon the injured archer among their company, that she might yet fight from the rear, and the party divided its meager treasures — a flask of alchemist's fire, a smokestick — among those with hands free to use them.

### **The Host of Sorrowful Stone**

Their road ran on through winding tunnels, past a harmless nest of spiders that scattered from the lantern-light, and at last into a wide carven hall. Along its walls, worked in patient bas-relief, stood a procession of crusaders in armor — yet none charged, none triumphed. Every graven face was bent with grief.

As **Rabiah** stepped close to read them, a patch of the ceiling darker than any shadow unfurled like a demon's umbrella and dropped upon her. A second fell upon Harlock. The creatures smothered their prey in inky wings and gnashing beaks, and the young sorcerer — who had strayed too far from Harlock's shadow — was seized and swallowed whole into a writhing leather sack, her cries falling suddenly silent. Blood ran from where the thing gripped her. She hung a hair's breadth from death when Harlock's hammer and Lupenor's arrows brought the beasts down; and as the dark things died, the unnatural gloom that clung to them dissolved, and the cavern brightened.

![The darkmantles fall from the grieving dark, and **Rabiah** is seized — while **Harlock's** hammer, **Lupenor's** bow, and the cold light of **Varic's** holy symbol turn to the fight.](images/the-host-of-sorrowful-stone.png)

Varic reached his fallen friend and, drawing on the mercy of his goddess, called Rabiah back from the very threshold — a ***rebuke of death*** that stole the last breath from oblivion's grasp. Channelled light and a healer's touch did the rest. The blind wizard of their company came groping to her side and laid ***mage armor*** upon her like a benediction. It was a hard lesson, gently given: the frail must not run ahead of the shield.

### **The Grief in the Carvings**

When the danger had passed, **Aravashnial** — sightless but keen of mind — bade the others describe the carved host to him, and grew feverish with excitement. These, he insisted, were the tainted crusaders of the First Crusade, corrupted in the depths of the Abyss, whose deformed children had been driven from the surface by fearful mobs. Down here, generations ago, they had descended in their hundreds to raise their misbegotten young in peace. He had suspected it for years, he said; here at last was proof. A faint reek of dung-smoke on the air told the searchers that someone still dwelt in these tunnels, and recently.

### **The Mad Warden of the Fallen Tower**

Beyond a chamber where a broken tower lay toppled on its side, they came upon a low campfire and a filthy, matted dwarf who shrieked at them in the tongue of dragons. This was **Millorn**, a wizard long unhinged by the corruption of the deep, and he fought with cruel cunning — hurling an enchanted dagger that returned to his hand, wrapping himself in a shimmering ***blur*** so that blades passed through empty air. A wash of ***color spray*** felled Lupenor senseless, and a summoned hound harried the party's flanks. Yet the madman had no answer for Harlock. He closed the distance and struck him down with a single crushing blow, and Millorn twisted out his last upon the sand. Among his hoard the company found a scrawled, half-mad manifesto no one could parse, a cloak that lent its wearer resilience, a curious bat brooch, and the dagger that bore his name in arcane script.

### **The Mongrels of the Deep**

A steep, piton-studded shaft rose before them, and it cost long, careful minutes to haul their blind and crippled charges up its face with rope and patient hands. At the top, in a cavern where another tower had collapsed, three horned and misshapen figures labored to dig a trapped companion from the rubble. Wary but not cruel, their leader **Lann** offered a bargain: pass in peace, or lend aid. The company chose aid. Straining together beneath a boulder that even Harlock could not shift alone, they freed the pinned wretch — a thing of tentacle and pincer and clustered eyes — and Varic mended what he could of the wretch's shattered leg.

![The company chooses to lend aid: **Harlock** sets his shoulder to the boulder beside the mongrelmen while **Varic** kneels to mend the freed wretch, his healing the one warm light in the dark.](images/the-mongrels-of-the-deep.png)

These were the mongrelmen, the very descendants of the carven host, and Lann pledged to guide his rescuers toward the surface. It fell to Rabiah's careful tongue to soothe the grumbling noble **Horgus**, whose disgust the mongrels bore in weary silence, while Aravashnial's fascination spilled over into thoughtless insult. Where the earth had torn itself into a great new chasm, the company crossed by the grace of a levitation charm, ferrying those who could not climb across the gulf.

### **The Sign of the Horned Lord**

In a round cavern choked with a hostile, spore-belching fungus, fire and oil cleared the path — and revealed two corpses that turned the day grim. They wore the tabards of the crusaders of **Iomedae**, yet one clutched an unholy symbol of **Baphomet**, the Horned Lord of the labyrinth. Infiltrators, then: traitors who might have walked freely into the city bearing that forbidden sign. Lann confirmed he had seen such men before, consorting with a wicked tribe of the deep. Aravashnial seized upon the token with a conspirator's grim triumph, vindicated at last — proof, he declared, that the **Templars of the Ivory Labyrinth** had wormed their way into the holy orders, and that the powers above must be warned. The company agreed to bear the bodies and that tainted symbol with them, that it might one day stand as evidence before the light. Wearier, wiser, and one dark revelation heavier, the company pressed on toward the surface and the burning city that awaited them.

## **The Children of the First Crusade**

<!-- fathom: 186092746 -->

*November 23, 2024 session — the mongrel village of Neathholm, deep beneath Kenabres, and the tunnels of the fallen tribe that guard the way upward*

### **The Crossing to Neathholm**

The road out of the dark had been long. Behind them lay a rescued huddle of mongrel folk pulled from a cave-in, a treacherous trench, and the corpses of men who had worn the seeming of crusaders but proved to be cultists in disguise. Now the guide called **Lann** led the company on through a thousand feet of limping, stumbling passage until the tunnel opened into a vast, moist cavern where water lapped unseen beneath the stone. Four misshapen sentinels rose with weapons half-drawn — and lowered them at Lann's cry of *peace*.

Beyond a narrow causeway, upon an island in a black underground lake, glimmered the candle-lights of **Neathholm**. Its dwellings were a hodgepodge of scavenged timber, stone, and one absurd patch of white picket fence, each hut as individual and ill-matched as the twisted people who had built them. **Harlock Greyforge**, **Lupenor Celest**, **Rabiah**, and **Varic Sarian** found themselves gazing on children as grotesque as their elders, peering from breaches in the walls. Even to hearts as forgiving as theirs, the sight was hard to bear — and every mongrel eye told them the feeling was mutual, and old.

![Beyond a narrow causeway, on an island in a black underground lake, glimmer the candle-lights of **Neathholm** — the hidden refuge of the mongrelfolk, walled in vast cavern dark, as the company crosses the still water toward it.](images/candle-lights-of-neathholm.png)

### **The Tale Chief Chusul Told**

At the largest hovel waited **Chusul**, chief of Neathholm: a bloated, rodent-faced man of one good eye and one clouded, a fine morningstar resting idle in a hand too weak to wield it well. He drew them inside, and there, when he learned that demons had reached **Kenabres** once more, he unfolded the sorrow of his people.

They were, he said, the children of the First Crusaders. His own forebear, the knight **Sir Cavius**, had marched against the **Worldwound** beside his wife; Cavius had fallen there, and his widow returned home carrying a child — but the passage through that wound in the world had corrupted the seed within her. The babe was born twisted, first of a new and hideous kind, and nearly slain at birth. She fled below and founded this refuge. So it had gone for a hundred years: crusaders' blood, once touched by the Worldwound, bred only ruin, even in unions with those who had never seen it. The surface had tolerated them out of awe and let them make their home in the deep. They lived short, painful lives — Chusul, at thirty, was the eldest among them — and few of their children drew breath at all. Their pride, he confessed, they kept in one thing only: the courage of the ancestors whose sacrifice had made them.

### **The Brooch of a Lost Son**

Among the spoils the company carried was a curious copper brooch, bat-shaped, taken from the corrupted dwarf they had slain in the dark. The mongrel merchant would not touch it; she pressed it back and bade them bring it to the chief. When **Lupenor** laid it before Chusul, the old man went still, then reached for it as though for a wound.

It had belonged to his son — the boy who had burned to charge the surface and take his place among heroes, who had defied his father's counsel and gone, and never returned. The dwarf must have taken it from his body. When Lupenor pressed the brooch back into his hand, Chusul in his grief gave the company his own morningstar in return — an heirloom of masterwork craft that had been carried into the very First Crusade by his ancestors — and asked only a promise: *beat up a demon with it.* Then he shuffled to his chamber, and the company withdrew, pretending not to hear him weep.

![By a single candle, **Lupenor** presses the copper brooch back into the trembling hands of **Chusul** — the keepsake of a son who marched to the surface and never returned. In his grief the old chief gave the company his own ancestral morningstar in return.](images/the-brooch-returned.png)

### **A Charge, and a Night's Rest**

Chusul asked no small thing. The mongrel tribes, a dozen scattered clans beneath the earth, stood ready to march and rejoin the crusade above — if only the company would carry that word to the surface, and clear the one road that led there. For a single tribe had fallen, seduced over ten slow years by the whispers and coin of demon-cultists, and now barred the way. He furnished them with healing draughts and restoratives from a little wooden box, and asked only that they cut down the leader of the fallen ones — who held court somewhere further down, in the deep. The rousing of the other clans he would see to himself, sending his own guards out to the scattered tribes once the road stood open.

The company took their rest in a curtained guest-room on beds of hide and feather, dining on lake-fish and pale mushroom while curious mongrel children watched through the gaps. The rest did more than mend their hurts; each of them woke steadier, surer of hand and will, as though the ordeals of the deep had quietly tempered them into something more than they had been. Their rescued surface companions would not be left behind: the scout **Anevia**, hobbled on a broken leg yet fierce to reach her wife above; the blind sage **Aravashnial**, who chafed at his darkened sight; and the haughty noble **Horgus Gwerm**, who reckoned the heroes his hired guards. They would follow, it was agreed, and hang back from the killing. In the quiet before sleep, a truth settled over them — they were no longer merely climbing toward daylight. They had a purpose now.

### **The Bull's-Head Road**

By the reckoning of a place without dawn, morning came. Lann guided them southeast to a pick-carved passage marked with a crude bull's head graven in the stone — the sigil of the demon whose cult had bought this tribe — and there he stopped, for this was as far as he would lead them. **Harlock** took the fore, the others in his wake.

At a shoulder-high barrier two mongrel guards leveled crossbows. **Varic** called out for parley; they answered with bolts. Harlock vaulted the breach and struck one down with a single crushing blow, and where his masterwork hammer fell the creature's chitinous arm cracked like an insect's shell. **Rabiah** loosed darts of force, and the first sentinel fell dying; the second, sheltered behind rubble, met the new heirloom morningstar in Lupenor's hands and then Harlock's follow-through, and did not rise. From behind a stone door came the sound of alarm.

### **Blood in the Warrens**

What followed was a running slaughter through the fallen tribe's dens. A stone door burst open on a snarling giant lizard and a knot of corrupted mongrels; Harlock hewed the beast while Lupenor and Varic loosed telling shots, and the room was cleared. In a common chamber a mongrel woman loosed arrows from behind an overturned chair, hiding to give herself what cover she could, until a bolt took her down. Beyond a foul, echoing door the company took the wiser path, dragging a heavy table across to bar whatever wallowed within, and pressed on.

A shield-bearing human cleric and a woman with a masterwork glaive made their stand in a trophy-hung room of preserved vermin; Harlock broke the cleric, and Varic's aim ended the reach-fighter before her long blade could reap. Among the spoils lay scrolls of *comprehend languages* and *cause fear*, a wand crackling with stored lightning that fell to Rabiah, and unholy relics the company left disdainfully behind.

At last two shafts descended into deeper dark — one plunging forty feet to rushing water, the other a drier drop of twenty. Blessed by Varic with ***guidance*** and a ***touch of destiny***, Harlock went down first, and found the fighting was not finished. More of the fallen swarmed below. There Harlock proved terrible in earnest: taking up the captured glaive, he loosed one appalling stroke that near-cleaved a mongrel man in two, while Rabiah dropped after him in a tumbler's roll and **Lupenor** and Varic thinned the ranks from above. The chamber quieted around them — but the fallen tribe's true master still waited, somewhere further down, in the deep.

## **The Dimmed Blade and the Burning Sky**

<!-- fathom: 194019062 -->

*December 7, 2024 session — beneath and above the stricken city of Kenabres*

### **In the Deep Beneath the City**

Far below **Kenabres**, where the mongrel-folk had kept their sunless warren for the better part of a century, the company pressed on through galleries of worked stone and raw cavern. Behind them, in a chamber judged safe, sheltered the frail souls they had drawn out of the dark: the proud lord **Horgus Gwerm**, who counted his gold even in ruin; the blind elven wizard **Aravashnial**, robbed of his sight; and **Anevia** of the **Eagle Watch**, wife to the knight **Irabeth**, hobbled by a broken leg. Ahead lay only doors, and the promise of the surface — if any of them could yet reach it.

Beyond a guarded threshold the way opened into a hall of the Horned Lord. Braziers burned with a low orange warmth over a long table set with a chair of honor, and a single darkened stain, old as a week and black as guilt, marred its center. Past it lay a reeking kitchen hung with the drying meat of butchered beasts. It was, the company understood, the heart of a cult that had bent its knee to **Baphomet**.

### **The Hall of the Horned Lord**

![**Rabiah**, youngest of the company, gathers the raw magic she looses into the sorceress — bolt upon bolt, across the reeking dark of the Horned Lord's hall.](images/rabiahs-raw-magic.png)

They found the cult's captains waiting. A sorceress in the corner raised a wand, and at her word a glaive of unholy force took shape in the empty air — a ***spiritual weapon*** that swung of its own malice at any who drew near. With a second word of ***Command*** she wrenched **Harlock Greyforge's** own glaive from his grip and sent it ringing to the stone. Beside her stood an armored brute who traded blows across the doorway that Harlock, faithful to **Iomedae**, refused to yield.

It was a grinding, close-quarters affair. **Varic Sarian** laid a ***Shield of Faith*** upon Harlock, then spent his channeled light to mend the company's hurts even as he feared to heal the foe with the same grace. **Rabiah**, youngest of them, loosed missile after missile of raw magic into the sorceress. **Lupenor Celest** slipped and stepped and loosed her shafts, and at the last drew steel and pressed into the chamber, taking wound upon wound until she stood a single breath from death. Yet the cult broke. The armored man fell, and the sorceress with him, her wicked Baphomet-marked knife slipping from her fingers. She was left dying, and no mercy is owed a servant of demons; **Lupenor** saw to it that she rose no more.

### **Radiance Reclaimed**

![**Harlock** lifts the dimmed sword ***Radiance*** from the cultists' box — dull, golden, and silent — half-fearing he has no right to a hero's blade.](images/the-dimmed-blade.png)

Among the spoils — a wand, potions, scrolls, a heathen prayer-book they nearly put to the flame — there stood a locked box, and within it a sword. Harlock knew it at once. Here was a blade of legend, cold iron and consecrated, borne long ago against the horrors of the **Worldwound** and stolen away by these very cultists to be defiled: ***Radiance***. Its light had guttered out through a hundred forgotten battles, and it lay now dull and golden and silent. Reverently Harlock took it up, half-fearing he had no right to it — for what was he but a plain swordsman, and not the holy champion such a relic deserved? Yet he vowed to see it returned one day to honest keeping, and consented, in a city crawling with the Abyss, to bear the dimmed thing at his side a while longer.

### **The Waters and the Maze**

Deeper still, a knotted rope carried them down a shaft into a cavern loud with a waterfall, where vicious fish thrashed in the black pool. Harlock descended first into the fight; **Lupenor**, drifting on the borrowed magic of ***levitation***, loosed arrows from the empty air above. Behind the falling water they pried open a hidden vault and lifted a dust-choked chest — a ring, an enspelled shield, coin and pearl and parchment to divide among them.

Higher galleries brought dire rats that fastened on Harlock and left the seed of sickness in his blood, which **Varic** tended with a healer's patient hand. Then the walls themselves turned cruel, folding into a maze — the very sigil of the demon-lord who ruled this cult. Out of it crawled the lesser fiends of the pit. One breathed a foul, reeling cloud that left Harlock retching and near-helpless, and **Lupenor** hauled him bodily from the murk while **Rabiah** steadied their courage with a ***Touch of Destiny***. Common steel could not pierce the fiends' hide; only cold iron told, bolt after bolt, until the last of them was undone — two left bubbling on the stone, and the third winking shrieking out of the world as it died, banished back to the Abyss that had spawned it.

### **The Sundered Sky**

![**Varic** claws his way up to his first open sky in days — and finds **Kenabres** broken above him, the heavens black and raining embers.](images/the-burning-sky.png)

At the maze's end they found the ladders to the world above, and climbed — and the world they climbed into was not the one they had left. **Kenabres** was broken. The sky hung black and raining embers; whole quarters had crumbled into chasms where the ground itself had torn open; screams and the guttural gibbering of demons carried on a wind that stank of burning. Winged shapes wheeled through the smoke overhead. In the days they had spent beneath the earth, the enemy had fallen upon the city, and the city had not held.

They did not flee. **Lupenor** stole across the rubble unseen and marked their place near the southern wall, and the company resolved to fight for whatever remained.

### **Through Ash to Sanctuary**

Crossing the shattered noble quarter, they were set upon by looters who took them for easy prey. Their leader wrapped the street in a shroud of ***darkness***, but it availed him nothing: **Harlock** hewed with steel and shield, his arm sure in the dark, while **Lupenor's** cold iron and **Rabiah's** magic found the thieves in the murk. All three were left where they fell.

At last, past an hour of clawing through wreckage, they reached the manor of **Horgus Gwerm** — untouched, by grim fortune, its master's fled servants having left nothing for the demons to break. The lord unlocked his own gate with his own hand, and the company searched the great house room by room before barring themselves within. There, in a stolen hour of safety, each of them felt the hard schooling of those days settle into something surer — Lupenor's eye quicker now to find the unguarded gap in a foe's defense, Rabiah's command of her gift broadened, Varic's prayers grown deeper, and Harlock's sword-arm steadier than before. Grown harder and stronger for all they had endured, they took what rest the night would grant, and turned their thoughts to the morning: to seek out **Anevia's** home and the halls of the city's great library, to find what crusaders yet lived, and to begin, street by ruined street, to take their city back.

*— Session of December 7, 2024 —*

## **The Long Road to the House of Tirabade**

<!-- fathom: 201534049 -->

*December 21, 2024 session — the ruin of **Kenabres**, in the days after the demon assault, as the company crosses the shattered city*

### **An Unnatural Dawn**

They woke in the borrowed comfort of **Horgus Gwerm**'s manor, upon feather beds behind velvet curtains — a strange luxury amid a world unmade. Beyond the walls the sky was wrong: a smear of rose light that faded to black as it climbed, choked with smoke and grit and the reek of sulfur, as though the **Worldwound** itself had reached up and swallowed **Kenabres** whole. In the foyer **Harlock Greyforge** cleaned and cleaned again the golden-bladed longsword taken from the mongrel dens below, until the polishing became a kind of prayer. Their host kept to some far corner of the manor, sleeping apart and asking no questions, though he saw them fed before they went. So the company took up their burdens — **Harlock**, the elven hunter **Lupenor Celest**, the young sorceress **Rabiah**, and **Varic Sarian**, priest of Sarenrae — and with the crippled **Anevia** and the blind mage **Aravashnial** in their care, they set out north through the corpse of the city toward **Anevia**'s home.

## **The Breaking of the Wardstone**

Rested and resolved, the company climbed back into the **Gray Garrison** to finish what they had begun. **Harlock** had carried the golden longsword out of that keep believing it only a fine relic. Six years she had grieved **Yaniel's** passing, withdrawn and silent — until this night, when something shattered and she woke to find that, at last, she could be heard. The company reasoned that the breaking of the **Wardstone** had loosed the power now humming in their blood, and that ***Radiance*** had drunk of it too.

There remained one riddle in the temple. A door of no visible hinge stood outlined upon a wall, sealed by more than lock.
