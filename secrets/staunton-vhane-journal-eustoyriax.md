# The Shadow's Gifts

*From the recovered journal of **Staunton Vhane** — on the coming of the shadow demon Eustoyriax and the Nahyndrian elixirs, late 4712 AR. Notes on an emissary from the Ivory Sanctum, and the price of ascension.*

### The Shadow's Arrival

*7th of Neth, 4712 AR*

Three weeks have passed since I came to Drezen. Three weeks since my seventy-four years of deception came crashing down around me in Kenabres.

I had grown careless. After so many decades leading the Hammers of Heaven, passing secrets to my Templar handlers, I had begun to believe myself untouchable. But that half-orc paladin — Irabeth — she was more cunning than I credited. She found the stolen missives. She found the holy relics I had been funneling to my masters. She confronted me in my own quarters, blade drawn, demanding answers I could not give.

I fled. There was nothing else to be done. I fled through the secret ways the Templars had prepared for just such an eventuality, and I made my way north to the only place that would have me — the citadel I had delivered into demonic hands so long ago.

Lady Aponavicius received me with what passes for warmth among her kind. "The traitor returns," she said, all six arms spread in mock welcome. "Your usefulness in Mendev is ended, but perhaps we can find other work for you here." She has made me welcome, though I sense she views me as a curiosity more than an asset — a dwarf who turned against his own kind, a paladin who found the courage to embrace what he truly was.

Today, an emissary arrived bearing gifts.

His name — or the name he gave — is Eustoyriax. A shadow demon of unusual power and cunning. He came to us wearing the body of a crusader named Maranse, a young knight-errant who must have had the misfortune of traveling alone through the Worldwound. The man's face still held traces of his former handsomeness, though his eyes had become pools of absolute darkness, and shadows clung to him like loyal hounds. When he spoke, his voice carried harmonics that no mortal throat could produce — the man's vocal cords moving, but something else shaping the words.

But it was not the possession that drew Lady Aponavicius's attention. It was the power radiating from him. Even I could feel it — a weight in the air, a darkness that seemed to drink the torchlight. This was no ordinary shadow demon. Something had changed him, elevated him beyond his kind. When he moved, the shadows in the room moved with him, bending toward him like flowers toward the sun. When he spoke, I felt the words resonating in my chest before I heard them with my ears.

Eustoyriax came bearing credentials from a place called the Ivory Sanctum — a fortress I had heard whispered of but never confirmed. He spoke of the Templars of the Ivory Labyrinth and of a master whose name he would not utter directly. More importantly, he came bearing gifts.

Five crystal vials, each containing a liquid the color of bruised midnight. He called them Elixirs of Distilled Power — brewed, he claimed, from powdered Nahyndrian crystal. I had heard the name before. The crystals form only in the deepest rifts of the Abyss, where reality itself bleeds. To powder such a thing, to distill it into something that could be consumed...

"You have already tasted this power yourself," Lady Aponavicius observed, her six eyes studying the shadow demon with naked interest. It was not a question.

Eustoyriax inclined Maranse's head. "The Ivory Sanctum rewards its faithful servants. I am proof of what the elixirs can bestow."

Lady Aponavicius held one of the vials to the torchlight, watching the liquid shift and move of its own accord within the crystal. "And what," she asked, her six arms arranged in that configuration I have learned means calculation, "does the Ivory Sanctum expect in return?"

Eustoyriax smiled with Maranse's face. "Merely that you remember who sent them, when the time comes."

*— S.V.*

### The Testing

*12th of Neth, 4712 AR*

Lady Aponavicius is no fool. She did not rise to command an army of demons by drinking unknown substances offered by strangers. Before she would consider using one of the Nahyndrian elixirs herself, she demanded proof of their efficacy — and their survivability.

"We shall test them," she announced, "on servants of varying constitution. If the elixirs kill, better they kill the expendable."

Eustoyriax did not object. He seemed, if anything, curious to observe the results.

I surprised myself by stepping forward.

"Let me be first," I said. The words left my mouth before I fully understood why I was speaking them. Lady Aponavicius turned all six of her eyes upon me — she has that disconcerting ability to focus each independently — and I felt the weight of her consideration.

"The dwarf volunteers," she said, and there was something like amusement in her voice. "How unexpected. Why?"

I told her the truth, or as much of it as I understood. I had betrayed my oaths, abandoned my god, and delivered good men to slaughter. I had done these things willingly, even eagerly. But I remained what I had always been — a mortal, with a mortal's limitations. If I was to serve the Abyss, I wished to serve it with power worthy of my conviction. If the elixir killed me, then I had simply overestimated my worth. If it transformed me...

Lady Aponavicius considered for a long moment. Then she handed me one of the vials.

"Drink," she said. "And we shall see what you become."

*— S.V.*

### Ascension

*13th of Neth, 4712 AR*

I am writing this with a hand that no longer trembles. It should tremble. After what I experienced, any sane creature would shake for the rest of its days. But I find that I am no longer entirely sane, and I no longer entirely care.

The elixir tasted of nothing and everything. Cold iron and burning copper. Grave dirt and newborn screams. The moment it touched my tongue, I understood that I had made either the best or the worst decision of my existence.

The pain came first — a scouring fire that started in my gut and spread outward through every vein, every nerve, every fiber of my being. I felt my soul being... rewritten. The divine spark that Torag had placed within me when I took my paladin's oath — that spark I had thought extinguished when I turned to darkness — I felt it being torn out by the roots and replaced with something else. Something vast. Something hungry.

I screamed. I am told I screamed for six hours without stopping, though I have no memory of most of it. What I remember is the moment the pain crested and broke, like a fever finally releasing its grip. And in that moment of release, I felt power pour into the spaces where my mortality had been carved away.

I can see in absolute darkness now. Not merely well — perfectly, as though darkness itself has become my ally. I can feel the life force of nearby creatures, sense their fear like heat against my skin. When I struck a training dummy with my warhammer this morning, the blow shattered not just the dummy but the stone pillar behind it — and I had been holding back.

Lady Aponavicius examined me afterward. "Mythic," she pronounced, and I heard genuine respect in her voice for the first time. "The elixir has awakened mythic power within you, antipaladin. You have become something more than mortal."

Eustoyriax watched from the shadows — literally, he had partially merged with them — and his borrowed face wore an expression of satisfaction. "The dwarf's soul was already corrupted," he observed. "Already committed to darkness. The elixir found fertile ground."

Four vials remained. Lady Aponavicius declared the test a success — and then, to my surprise, took one for herself.

I will not presume to describe in detail the transformation of a marilith. It is not my place, and frankly, I spent much of it pressed against the far wall of the chamber, praying to powers I no longer believe in that she would not destroy us all in her thrashing. What I can say is this: Lady Aponavicius emerged from her ordeal greater. Her scales now shimmer with an inner darkness. Her six blades move faster than mortal eyes can track. And when she speaks, I feel the words in my chest before I hear them with my ears. She has ascended, as I have ascended. We are something more now.

Three vials remained.

*— S.V.*

### Record of Subsequent Tests

**Veraxalim — young blue dragon.** One of Lady Aponavicius's draconic servants, a cunning beast who had served her for three decades. He drank the elixir eagerly, having witnessed my transformation. The dragon's death took four hours. The Nahyndrian essence reacted catastrophically with his draconic blood — some fundamental incompatibility between the crystal's Abyssal origin and the dragon's innate magical nature. His scales cracked and leaked a substance that was neither blood nor ichor but something that dissolved whatever it touched. By the end, the dragon had liquefied from within, his roars becoming gurgles, then silence. We lost three servants to the splashing when he finally collapsed.

**The Xacarba — name unpronounceable.** A serpentine demon with three heads, each capable of independent thought. This was the very creature Lady Aponavicius had assigned to guard the Sword of Valor in the dungeons below — a colossal serpent coiled around the banner in eternal darkness. It volunteered eagerly, believing its nature as a creature of the Abyss would make it compatible with Abyssal crystal. It was wrong. The xacarba's three heads began arguing with each other almost immediately after consumption — not their usual disagreements, but genuine conflict, as though each head had become a separate entity entirely. Within an hour, the heads were attempting to devour each other. The body convulsed so violently that the spine shattered. When the thrashing finally stopped, the three heads continued snapping and biting at empty air for another day before finally falling still.

> **Note:** The xacarba's colossal corpse remains where it fell, in the dungeon depths near the Sword of Valor. Lady Aponavicius saw no reason to move it. "Let it serve as a warning," she said, "to any who would seek the banner."

**Soltengrebbe — mythic chimera.** A chimera of exceptional size and ferocity. Unlike the others, Soltengrebbe did not volunteer — it was forced to drink by its handlers, six of whom died in the attempt. The result was survival. The chimera emerged from its transformation even more terrible than before. Its lion head can now breathe fire that burns even in water. Its dragon head's breath has become a cone of pure shadow that drains the life from whatever it touches. Its goat head... speaks now. In Abyssal. It whispers prophecies of doom that have, so far, proven disturbingly accurate. Lady Aponavicius has taken Soltengrebbe as her personal mount.

### The Shadow Remains

*3rd of Kuthona, 4712 AR*

The testing is complete. Of the five elixirs, two empowered (myself and Lady Aponavicius), two destroyed (the dragon and the xacarba), and one transformed the chimera Soltengrebbe into something even more terrible than before.

Lady Aponavicius is not pleased.

The xacarba was dear to her — a servant of many decades, cunning and loyal in equal measure. It had guarded the Sword of Valor in the dungeons below since the citadel's fall, coiled around the banner in eternal darkness. Now its colossal corpse rots in those same depths, and the banner lies unguarded.

"Your gift cost me a valued servant," Lady Aponavicius said to Eustoyriax, her voice cold as Abyssal iron. "The elixir that was meant to elevate my xacarba instead destroyed it. You will replace what your gift has taken."

The shadow demon did not protest. Indeed, I thought I saw something like satisfaction flicker across Maranse's borrowed features.

"The banner must not be reclaimed," Lady Aponavicius continued. "It cannot be destroyed, and it cannot be corrupted. But it can be guarded. You will dwell in the chamber below, shadow demon. You will wrap yourself around that cursed standard and ensure that no crusader ever lays hands upon it again. This is the price of your gift."

Eustoyriax bowed low — a mockery of courtly grace performed with a dead man's body. "It would be my pleasure," he said. "I am patient, Lady. And patience is rewarded. The crusaders will come for their precious banner eventually. When they do, I wish to be there to greet them."

He smiled with Maranse's face — a smile that no longer looked even remotely human. I suspect he wanted this assignment all along. A mythic shadow demon, dwelling in perfect darkness, guarding a light that must never shine again. Whatever game the Ivory Sanctum plays, Eustoyriax has positioned himself exactly where he wishes to be.

*— S.V.*

### Addendum

*15th of Kuthona, 4712 AR — added later, in a different ink*

Maranse is dead. Lady Aponavicius wished to reward the succubus Kiranda for her work corrupting a crusader captain in Kenabres, and Eustoyriax offered his host body as a gift. "I have worn it long enough," he said. "The flesh grows tedious." Kiranda kept the man alive for three days. What remains of him is not worth describing.

Eustoyriax now moves through the lower chambers as pure shadow, darker than the darkness around him. He seems... pleased with the arrangement. I do not like having him beneath my feet, but I like even less the thought of what might happen if the crusaders ever reach his chamber. For their sake, I hope they never do.

*— S.V.*
