# Varic's Dream

*The mythic dream that came to **Varic Sarian** in the wake of the Wardstone's breaking.*

### The Dawnflower's Embrace

*Exhausted from the battle at the Gray Garrison, you collapse onto your bedroll. As consciousness slips away, the lingering heat of battle still burns in your veins...*

You are seven years old again, clutching your mother's hand as you walk through a bustling marketplace in Augustana. The scent of salt water mingles with exotic spices as merchants call out their wares. Your father haggles over the price of textiles, his voice confident but kind. You watch, absorbing every detail of the transaction, though your eyes constantly wander to the glittering bay beyond.

"Pay attention, Varic," your father says with a gentle smile. "Someday this will all be yours."

The scene shifts.

You're in a small cart, bouncing along a coastal road. Your mother points out fishing villages nestled against the shoreline, explaining how their family business connects city dwellers to these humble providers. Your father tells stories of his own childhood, of learning to respect both coin and those who earn it through honest labor.

Then screams. The cart jerks violently. Your mother throws herself over you as arrows pierce the canvas. Your father's voice rises in desperate plea, then falls silent forever.

Rough hands pull you from beneath your mother's lifeless body. Through tear-blurred eyes, you see the bandits arguing over what to do with you. One raises a blade.

White-hot rage fills you – your first taste of fury – a child's helpless, consuming anger.

A horn sounds in the distance. The bandits look up in alarm. "The temple guard," one hisses, and they flee, leaving you alone with the dead. As you kneel beside your parents' bodies, the setting sun casts long shadows across the road, painting the sky in hues of vengeful red.

Light surrounds you – golden, warm, unbearably bright. Standing before you is a figure in simple robes, her hands outstretched in compassion.

"The child lives," she says simply, and reaches for you.

The memory blurs, and now you're kneeling in a modest chapel. Sunlight streams through simple stained glass. An elderly priestess of Sarenrae places a weathered hand upon your head.

"The Dawnflower did not save you without purpose, child," she tells you. "Her light heals the wounded and illuminates the path to redemption for all."

"Even for them?" you ask, your young voice tight with grief. "For those who killed my parents?"

Her eyes are gentle but firm. "Especially for them, who need it most."

The words taste like ash in your mouth.

The dream shifts, and suddenly you stand on the temple's roof at dawn. You are seventeen, dressed in the white robes of an initiate. This is the day of your Path-Choosing – "The Dawn's Reflection." Fellow acolytes and senior priests gather in a semicircle, their faces solemn yet expectant.

Before you on a marble altar rest the sacred objects representing Sarenrae's domains: a polished bronze mirror for Glory, a clay bowl of healing herbs for Healing, a brazier of purifying flame for Fire, a sunrise-colored key for Redemption, a finely crafted scimitar for War, and a crystal prism for Sun.

The High Dawnflower approaches, her aged face serene in the first light of day. She anoints your forehead with sacred oil that catches the sunrise and seems to glow against your skin.

"Approach each aspect of the goddess," she instructs. "Let your spirit find its resonance with hers."

The temple musicians begin a soft, melodic hymn as you step toward the first object – the clay bowl of Healing. As your fingers touch its rim, you feel a gentle warmth, a sense of purpose. You've always excelled in the healing arts, finding satisfaction in mending wounds and easing pain.

You move to the mirror of Glory. In its reflection, you momentarily see not just yourself, but the faces of your parents, smiling with pride. Your heart swells with determination to honor their memory through worthy deeds.

Next, you approach the brazier of Fire. The flames leap unexpectedly as you near, dancing higher, more intensely. Heat washes over your face, and within the fire, you glimpse visions of righteous vengeance – bandits cowering before your wrath, cultists burning for their sins. The intensity of your reaction startles you, and more troubling still, you feel not horror but satisfaction at these visions.

You step back, unnerved by your own response. The High Dawnflower watches with knowing eyes but says nothing.

You move quickly past the remaining objects, barely registering their presence. When you kneel before the High Dawnflower, your decision feels both right and incomplete.

"I choose the paths of Healing and Glory," you declare, your voice steady despite the lingering unease.

The High Dawnflower nods and presents you with the sacred vows:

"I shall be the tender dawn that soothes the night's wounds, bringing comfort to those who suffer in darkness."

"I shall be the light that reveals the greatness of the Dawnflower to all who witness her works through me."

You speak the words with conviction, yet as you do, you notice your shadow cast long across the temple floor by the brazier's flames – reaching toward the fire you've chosen to deny.

The High Dawnflower places the white healing sash over your left shoulder and hangs the golden medallion of Glory around your neck. But as she steps back, she pauses, studying your face.

"The paths we choose shape our service," she says quietly, for your ears alone. "But they do not change our nature. The fire that burns within you will find its purpose, whether channeled through sacred rites or left to smolder unattended."

The scene dissolves, years compress into moments. You see yourself healing the sick, spreading Sarenrae's teachings, performing the duties of your chosen paths with diligence. Yet in moments of quiet reflection, you often find yourself staring into flames – campfires, temple candles, the sunset's burning hues – wondering about the path not taken.

Then Kenabres. The demon attack. The ruined streets where you and your new companions hunt cultists and demons alike. You heal Harlock's wounds after he shows mercy to yet another enemy who deserves far worse in your estimation.

The Gray Garrison looms before you. Unlikely allies stand at your side. Together, you push forward, challenging the impossible. The caged wardstone pulses with energy as you attack its defender, buying time for Rabiah. Power unlike anything you've known floods through you.

And then stillness.

You stand in a vast desert under a night sky filled with impossibly bright stars. Before you, the sands begin to glow. Dawn breaks, not from the horizon, but from within the earth itself. The sand shifts and rises, forming the figure of a woman of breathtaking beauty. Her skin radiates golden light, her eyes contain the fire of suns yet unborn.

Sarenrae, the Dawnflower, stands before you. At her feet lie the objects from your Path-Choosing ceremony so many years ago.

"My faithful Varic," her voice is like the first birdsong at daybreak. "You chose your paths with a divided heart."

She approaches, and the air around you warms like a perfect summer morning.

"You have served well on the paths of Healing and Glory. Your hands have mended the broken, your deeds have honored my name. But your eyes have never stopped watching the flame."

She gestures to the brazier of Fire, which burns more brightly than the others.

"You turned from this path not from lack of calling, but from fear of your own nature. You thought to deny the fire of righteous anger by refusing to channel it. Yet it burns in you still, seeking purpose."

She leans forward and places a kiss upon your brow. It fills you with warmth that reaches to your very core.

"The mythic power awakened within you seeks wholeness, not division. Against the Worldwound's corruption, all aspects of my light are needed – the healing touch, the glorious example, and yes, the purifying flame."

She steps back, her form shimmering like heat above desert sand.

"Remember your Path-Choosing, how the flame responded to your presence. That was not coincidence, but recognition. The time for fear has passed."

As she begins to fade, another figure materializes – a woman in gleaming armor, bearing the symbols of Iomedae. Though less radiant than Sarenrae, her presence carries undeniable authority.

"Varic Sarian," Iomedae's voice resonates with martial clarity. "I thank you for your courage at the Gray Garrison and for preventing my wardstone from becoming a weapon against the very crusaders it was meant to protect."

She approaches, her armor gleaming with divine light.

"Your goddess speaks truth. Against the corruption of the Abyss, half measures will not suffice. Honor all aspects of your divine calling, not just those that come easily to your nature."

She touches your shoulder briefly, her gauntleted hand surprisingly gentle.

"The Worldwound demands healers who understand that some wounds can only be cleansed with fire."

As both divine figures fade into the growing dawn, their final words reach you not as sound but as understanding blossoming within:

"Return to the Path-Choosing. What was left unfinished can now be completed."

You wake with the first light of dawn upon your face, your holy symbol warm against your chest. For a moment, you swear you can smell temple incense and feel the weight of a decision long deferred. The paths you chose have served you well, but perhaps now – with mythic power flowing through your veins – it is time to embrace what you once feared to acknowledge.

You know, with a certainty that defies all logic, that in this holy moment of dawn's first light, you have the opportunity to speak the vow that frightened you so long ago: "I shall be the purifying flame that consumes corruption and burns away falsehood, leaving only truth"
