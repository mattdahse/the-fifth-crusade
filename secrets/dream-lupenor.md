# Lupenor's Dream

*The mythic dream that came to **Lupenor Celest** in the wake of the Wardstone's breaking.*

### Threads of Fate

*You collapse into exhausted sleep after the battle at the Gray Garrison, your body aching from wounds both seen and unseen. As consciousness fades, memories of recent revelations and ancient mysteries swirl together in your mind...*

You are eight years old, sitting cross-legged in a corner of your family's trading post. Merchants of House Celest move around you in a choreographed dance of commerce, their voices a constant melody of negotiation. Your fingers work at the simple lock your cousin gave you, trying to feel for the tumblers as he taught you.

"Lupenor," your mother's voice cuts through the din. She stands over you, her blonde-red hair—so like your own—framing her disapproving face. "A merchant's daughter has no need for such... skills."

She confiscates the lock, but later that night, your father slips it back to you with a conspiratorial wink.

"Knowledge is never wasted," he whispers. "The Houses play their games, little one. Best to understand all the rules, even those they don't speak aloud."

The scene shifts. You are twelve, trailing behind your father through the back alleys of a border town. He meets with figures who don't come to the front door of respectable establishments. You're supposed to wait outside, but your elven ears catch whispers of "alternative import routes" and "unofficial taxation." When questioned later, your father simply says, "Trade flows like water, finding every possible path. Remember that."

That night, you practice with your small bow until your fingers bleed, imagining yourself a protector of caravans rather than their bookkeeper.

The dream blurs. You're fifteen, and your father catches you picking the lock to his private study.

Instead of anger, his face shows resignation. "Your mother wanted a merchant," he sighs. "I see something else in you."

"I can be both," you insist.

"Perhaps." He opens the door himself, reveals ledgers with two distinct accounting systems. "House Celest survives by understanding the spaces between realms, Lupenor. But be careful which spaces you step into."

He teaches you both sides of the business then—the legitimate face and the necessary compromises. You excel at both, but it's the shadows that call to you, the thrill of moving unseen, of knowing secrets others don't.

The scene shifts again. You're eighteen, on your first independent trading mission along the borders of the Worldwound. Something goes terribly wrong—a miscalculation, a betrayal. You find yourself alone, separated from your caravan, somehow deep in enemy territory.

Terror grips you as you hide beneath a fallen tree, watching demonic patrols pass mere feet away. Three days you survive like this, moving only at night, using every skill your father taught you—both the respectable and the shadowed.

On the third night, as dretches close in on your position, a woman appears beside you. Her beauty is striking yet unsettling, her movements possessing an otherworldly grace. Around her neck, barely visible in the darkness, hangs a small symbol that reminds you of stars. Something about her feels both dangerous and comforting, like standing at the edge of a precipice with the certainty you won't fall.

Without speaking, she guides you through impossible passages, teaching you to move between heartbeats of danger.

"Why are you helping me?" you finally ask.

"Even in the darkest places, change is possible," she replies cryptically, eyes momentarily flashing with something that might be regret or hope. "Some of us seek second chances."

Before she vanishes into the night, she looks at you with an intensity that burns itself into your memory. "The Songbird of the Spheres sees what others miss. In the spaces between certainties, she offers not order, but beautiful chaos. Perhaps you'll understand someday."

You return to House Celest changed. Your mother notices your newfound stillness, the way you observe before speaking, the way locks seem to open at your approach. Your father recognizes something more—a shadow of something unknowable.

"You've been touched by something beyond the ordinary," he murmurs one evening. "Some call it luck, but luck is just another name for forces we don't yet understand."

The dream shifts. You stand before House Celest's council, your trading record impeccable, your methods occasionally questioned but results never denied.

"The Worldwound expedition requires a representative," the head of the house announces. "Someone who understands... alternative approaches."

You're selected not for your merchant skills, but for those you've cultivated in shadow—observation, stealth, precision. The realization brings both pride and a strange sense of loss. Did you choose this path, or were you nudged along it by circumstances, by an encounter that sometimes feels more dream than memory?

Years compress into fragments. You excel in your role for House Celest, becoming known for handling delicate situations with unusual methods. Your bow becomes as much a tool of your trade as your ledger. You navigate dangerous territories with uncanny luck, always finding the safe path.

Yet something nags at you—a sense of playing a role written by someone else. Your caution in battle, your reluctance to step into danger unless absolutely necessary... is it wisdom or something else? Sometimes in quiet moments, you find yourself looking at the stars, feeling a strange connection to them you can't explain.

The scene shifts to recent days. The attack on Kenabres. Chaos erupting as demons pour from the sky, the city burning. You find yourself fighting alongside strangers who quickly become allies.

Your skills guide them through the ruined city's shadows, your bow finds targets with precision, locks yield to your touch. For once, you're not merchant or spy or house representative—just a survivor using every tool at your disposal.

Then comes the discovery that shatters your foundation. In the aftermath of a battle, beneath the rubble of what was once a cultist safe house, you find a courier. He wears House Celest's livery, and in his satchel are bills of sale for unholy symbols of Baphomet. Your family's house—stalwart allies of the crusade, or so you believed—implicated in trafficking with the enemy.

The shock reverberates through you as you continue the fight, every certainty suddenly questionable. Who among your house knew? How deep does the corruption run? Was your mysterious savior years ago somehow connected?

The Gray Garrison looms before you. Infiltrating its halls with your new companions, you find yourself instinctively falling back on old habits—staying to the shadows, finding the safe position, letting others take the greater risks.

Then the final chamber. The wardstone—Iomedae's sacred barrier against the Worldwound—stands caged at the center of the room, guarded by a lone oracle wielding a scythe. Jeslyn, they call her.

Events unfold with the precision of a well-rehearsed play. Harlock and Varic charge forward, drawing the guardian's attention. Rabiah, already invisible, waits in the shadows. You enter with the mongrelmen rangers, positioning yourself at a safe distance, bow at the ready.

Arrows fly. Your first shot finds its mark in Jeslyn's shoulder. The mongrelmen loose their own volley. Harlock presses the attack, his blade seeking weaknesses in the oracle's defense. Varic's prayers fill the air with golden light. All the while, the invisible Rabiah cautiously probes the cage surrounding the wardstone, searching for its weakness.

The guardian staggers under your combined assault, blood flowing from multiple wounds. Victory seems imminent. Then a flash of movement—Rabiah becoming visible as she strikes the wardstone with a rod of cancellation.

Light explodes outward. The wardstone fractures into a million glittering shards. The explosion pulverizes Jeslyn where she stands and sends waves of energy washing over you all. You try to move, to speak, but find yourself frozen, enraptured. The life of the wardstone flashes through your mind—decades of power and purpose, now released.

The mongrelmen footmen panic, trying in vain to rouse you from this trance. But you're lost in the vision, suspended between moments.

A shimmering image forms in the center of the room—a woman of terrible beauty and malevolent purpose. Areelu Vorlash, architect of the Worldwound itself. Her projected form speaks words of threat and triumph. With a casual gesture, she casts a spell—mass suffocation—that drops the mongrelmen to their knees, gasping for air.

But you and your companions remain unaffected, still locked in communion with the wardstone's dying energy. Frustration flashes across Vorlash's face. She tears open reality itself, creating a gate to the Abyss. Through it pour six babau demons, their lanky forms glistening with acidic slime, longspears clutched in their clawed hands. Behind them, countless more demons wait to follow.

Just then, the trance breaks. Power surges through you—not just through you, but from you. From all four of you. It rushes outward in a wave of pure force, slamming the gate closed and tearing through Vorlash's projected image. She screams, her form dissolving into motes of light.

But the demons already through remain. They raise their hands, chanting in their foul tongue, and three more of their kind materialize in bursts of sulfurous smoke. Nine babau demons now surround you, their weapons leveled, their hunger palpable.

You immediately fall back to your position near the wall, drawing arrow after arrow, each shot finding its mark in demonic flesh. The creatures divide, surrounding each of you.

Three demons converge on you, their longspears thrusting from multiple directions. You dodge one, deflect another with your bow, but the third catches you across the arm, leaving a burning gash. Backed against the wall, nowhere left to retreat, you continue firing at point-blank range. One demon falls, a second staggers back with an arrow through its throat.

Across the chamber, something unexpected happens. Rabiah—slight, unarmored Rabiah—grips a morningstar in her uncalloused hand. With a cry that sounds more like joy than fear, she leaps onto the empty plinth where the wardstone once stood. From this elevated position, she alternates between cracking demon skulls with her borrowed weapon and hurling arcane fire from her fingertips.

The sight leaves you momentarily stunned. This wisp of a girl, whom you'd assumed would need protection, fights with abandon, with a wild courage that makes your careful positioning seem suddenly... inadequate.

The third demon lunges at you. You sidestep, but find yourself surrounded again as another breaks off from the group attacking Harlock. Four glowing eyes fix on you with malevolent hunger. Your quiver grows light—only a few of the special arrows remain.

Something shifts inside you. A realization, perhaps, or a question: What if caution is sometimes just another word for fear?

You stop backing away. Plant your feet. Draw and release with fluid precision that seems to flow from somewhere beyond conscious thought. Demons fall. Your last arrow flies. When a babau lunges, you drive an arrow directly into its eye with your bare hand.

As the last demon falls, you find yourself breathing hard, bleeding from multiple wounds, yet feeling more alive than you can remember. The mythic power awakened by the wardstone courses through your veins, whispering of possibilities yet unexplored.

Then stillness.

You stand in a vast meadow beneath an impossibly star-filled sky. Before you materializes a woman of otherworldly beauty. Her hair shifts like the night sky, her eyes contain the light of distant stars, and butterfly wings unfurl from her back.

Desna, the Song of the Spheres, stands before you.

"My cautious Lupenor," her voice sounds like wind through tall grass. "You who have walked the edge between shadow and light, between watching and acting."

She approaches, and the air around you fills with the scent of night-blooming flowers.

"The encounter that saved you years ago was no accident, though perhaps not entirely as it seemed. Some who serve me seek redemption for past deeds. Through such unexpected messengers, fate finds its winding path."

Her gaze seems to peer through you, seeing every doubt that now plagues you.

"In the wardstone chamber, you witnessed what happens when necessity overcomes habit. The young sorceress showed you a different way of facing danger—not reckless, but embracing risk with open eyes. Did you feel it then? The stirring of something long dormant?"

She leans forward and places a kiss upon your forehead. It feels like the first light of dawn breaking through night's final hour.

"Your caution has kept you alive through many dangers, but the mythic power awakened within you calls for more. It asks not for recklessness, but for bold purpose. You survived today not by finding the safest corner, but by standing your ground when no safe corner remained."

She steps back, starlight swirling around her form.

"The corruption you've discovered in your house is but one thread in a greater tapestry. Your eyes, trained to see value where others see only cost, will be needed in the days ahead. But so too will your arrows and your courage."

As she begins to fade, another figure materializes—a woman in gleaming armor, bearing the symbols of Iomedae. Though less radiant than Desna, her presence carries undeniable authority.

"Lupenor Celest," Iomedae's voice resonates with quiet strength. "I thank you for your courage at the Gray Garrison and for your role in preventing my wardstone from becoming a weapon against the crusaders it was meant to protect."

She approaches, her scarred but beautiful face solemn.

"You stand at a crossroads. The skills you learned in shadow will serve the light if you choose it. The caution that has preserved your life need not prevent you from living it fully."

She touches your shoulder, her gauntleted hand surprisingly gentle.

"The discovery you've made about your house troubles you deeply, as it should. But remember—sometimes we must question everything we thought we knew to find a greater truth. Your merchant's eye for detail and your warrior's instinct for survival will both be needed on the path ahead."

As both divine figures fade into the star-filled night, their final words reach you like a breath of wind:

"The merchants of House Celest trade in certainties. But you, daughter of fortune, have witnessed the power of possibility. Which will you choose?"

You wake with tears streaming down your face, your hand instinctively touching the quiver at your side—counting arrows as if to reassure yourself they're there. The memory of standing your ground, surrounded by demons, lingers with unexpected sweetness. For the first time in memory, the prospect of danger ahead fills you not with calculated caution, but with a wild, unfamiliar excitement. The mythic power coursing through your veins seems to whisper: your luck was never meant to be conserved, but spent in pursuit of wonders yet unseen.
