# The Corruption Forge

*From the recovered journal of **Staunton Vhane** — a register of holy relics unmade at the Corruption Forge beneath Drezen, 4673–4675 AR. A chronicle of relics remade in service to our new masters.*

### Item the First: Dawn's Mercy → Dusk's Cruelty

*Completed 3rd of Gozran, 4673 AR*

**Provenance.** A longsword carried by Paladin-Commander Sullus during the liberation of Iz. Blessed by the hand of Iomedae herself — or so the inscription claimed.

**Before.** The blade shone with holy light bright as morning sun. It dealt grievous harm to undead and fiendish creatures, and the wielder could channel positive energy through their strikes, healing allies even as enemies were cut down.

**After.** Now shrouded in shadow that devours nearby light. It inflicts agonizing wounds upon celestials and good-aligned mortals, and channels negative energy that withers flesh and feeds the wielder's strength. Lady Aponavisius awarded this to Captain Rhorrak for the siege of Pulura's Fall.

### Item the Second: Aegis of the Martyr → Carapace of the Tyrant

*Completed 18th of Gozran, 4673 AR*

**Provenance.** Full plate armor worn by Saint Clydwell of the First Crusade, who famously stood alone against a balor for three hours while civilians evacuated the temple district of Sarkoris.

**Before.** The steel never tarnished, always gleaming silver-white. It granted divine protection against evil magics, and when the wearer defended another, their wounds would close with supernatural speed — the armor seemed to draw suffering unto itself and transform it into healing light.

**After.** The plates have blackened and grown serrated edges that weep slowly. It grants unholy protection against good-aligned magic, and now leeches vitality from nearby allies to heal the wearer — those within ten paces feel a constant cold exhaustion as their life force is siphoned away. The armor feeds upon loyalty and twists it into parasitic dependence. Joran wears this himself now. He has grown... larger in it.

### Item the Third: Redeemer's Gauntlets → Chains of Damnation

*Completed 9th of Desnus, 4673 AR*

**Provenance.** Forged by the celestial smith Azaeriel as a gift to the mortal priestess Serastes. She used them to cure the Plaguebringer's victims during the siege of Kenabres in the Second Crusade.

**Before.** White steel lined with cloth-of-gold. The wearer could lay hands upon the afflicted and burn away curses, diseases, and possessions. Thrice daily they could channel purifying energy that would restore even those on death's threshold.

**After.** The steel has tarnished to a sickly grey-green, the gold lining rotted to black. Now the touch inflicts terrible curses that twist mind and body, and can spread disease with a mere brush of the fingers. The purifying channel has inverted — now it marks victims for death, ensuring lingering suffering. Currently held in reserve for interrogations. Most effective.

### Item the Fourth: Liberator's Longbow → Slaver's Arbalest

*Completed 21st of Desnus, 4673 AR*

**Provenance.** Carried by the ranger Kelthus Freewind, who earned his name by liberating twelve slave caravans bound for the Worldwound. The wood was said to be from Elysium itself.

**Before.** A composite longbow of silverwood. Arrows fired from it would bypass any defense when shot to free captives or protect the helpless, and it granted its wielder supernatural freedom of movement — no binding could hold them, no impediment slow them.

**After.** Reformed into a heavy crossbow of twisted blackthorn. Its bolts now entangle and paralyze victims, wrapping them in spectral chains, and the wielder can dominate the will of those they have wounded, making slaves of enemy and former ally alike. The freedom of movement has inverted — the wielder moves freely while all others find themselves increasingly constrained. Sent north to the slave markets at Undarin.

### Item the Fifth: Compassion's Ward → Spite's Bulwark

*Completed 7th of Sarenith, 4674 AR*

**Provenance.** A shield carried by Haverik the Pious during the Second Crusade. He famously used it to shelter orphans during the Siege of Keeper's Gorge, standing over them for two days while demons battered against its magic.

**Before.** A heavy steel shield that gleamed with a soft rose-gold light. It would reflect harm back upon any who struck at those unable to defend themselves, and emanated an aura of sanctuary that protected children and innocents within its presence — demons found themselves unable to approach.

**After.** The metal has corroded to rust-red iron. Now it reflects pain manifold upon any who dare strike the wielder, regardless of cause or justice. The sanctuary has become a field of terror — all nearby creatures feel a creeping dread and paranoid fear, turning ally against ally. Children who approach it have nightmares for weeks after. (This pleased Aponavisius greatly. She called it "poetry.") Currently mounted on the gates of the lower dungeons.

### Item the Sixth: Truthseeker's Brand → Deceiver's Maul

*Completed 19th of Sarenith, 4674 AR*

**Provenance.** A warhammer commissioned by Inquisitor Daarken of Lastwall. Used to root out corruption and lies within the crusader ranks during the Third Crusade.

**Before.** The hammer's head bore the scales of justice in gold inlay. It would glow blue-white in the presence of deception, growing brighter the greater the falsehood. Its wielder could sense lies as easily as others sense heat, making them nearly impossible to mislead.

**After.** The gold has become lead, the scales tipped permanently to one side. Now it obscures truth rather than revealing it — in the wielder's presence, lies become believable and truth sounds like madness. It grants an uncanny ability to craft deceptions and don false identities. Several of our spies in Kenabres now carry blessed copies; the originals Aponavisius keeps for her own agents infiltrating the crusader command.

### Item the Seventh: Vanguard's Resolve → Breaker of Wills

*Completed 7th of Erastus, 4674 AR*

**Provenance.** A greatsword passed through seven commanders of the Second Shield Wall. Last wielded by Commander Rhestine before her fall.

**Before.** A cold iron blade inscribed with Celestial oaths. It projected an aura of courage that banished fear and exhaustion, amplified the bearer's commands across the battlefield, and could invoke a blessing that inspired allies to legendary heroism.

**After.** Corrupted to black steel graven with Abyssal script. It projects a crushing despair that breaks a warrior's resolve, amplifies cruelty and condemnation, and its invocation now causes a paralyzing fear that sends even veterans fleeing. Awarded to General Khorramzadeh for the assault on the Fourth Shield Wall.

### Item the Eighth: Mantle of Sacred Duty → Shroud of Profane Oath

*Completed 23rd of Erastus, 4674 AR*

**Provenance.** A cloak worn by the oath-bound knights of Iomedae during their vigil ceremonies. This particular mantle belonged to the order's grand master, Sir Braeven the Uncompromising.

**Before.** A white cloak that never stained despite any hardship. It protected the wearer from any compulsion to commit evil acts — no magic or torture could force them to break their sacred oaths — and its immunity to fear made them stalwart in any circumstance.

**After.** The white has become a grey so dark it drinks in light. Now it prevents the wearer from being swayed from evil purpose — no appeal to mercy or reason can turn them aside once committed to a dark deed — and it radiates an aura of despair that saps hope from all nearby. The wearer becomes inevitable as death itself. Brother Joran refuses to enter the same room as this item. I find it... comforting.

### Item the Ninth: Guardian's Greaves → Stalker's Stride

*Completed 11th of Arodus, 4675 AR*

**Provenance.** Boots worn by the champion Veralik, known for his impossible speed in coming to the defense of endangered villagers. During the Siege of Drezen he personally rescued eighty-three civilians from behind enemy lines in a single night.

**Before.** Enchanted boots that granted preternatural speed when rushing to defend others in peril. They left blessed footprints that would heal the minor wounds of those who walked through them, and the ground itself seemed eager to bear the wearer toward righteous purpose.

**After.** Now they grant unholy speed when pursuing fleeing prey or victims attempting escape. Their footprints leave cursed earth that causes stumbling and weakness in any who follow, and the ground itself seems to reach up and grasp at those the wearer hunts. Particularly effective for running down deserters or escapees. Three of our hunter-squads now wear these.

### Item the Tenth: Helm of the Righteous → Crown of the Damned

*Completed 29th of Arodus, 4675 AR*

**Provenance.** A helmet forged for Watcher-Captain Harren, commander of Kenabres' night watch. Used to detect demonic infiltrators and protect against mental domination during the dark years following the First Crusade's collapse.

**Before.** A silver helm that granted the ability to see through all illusions and disguises once per day. It protected the wearer's mind from possession, domination, and other forms of mental assault, and made them nearly impossible to deceive or control.

**After.** The silver has blackened to iron crowned with twisted horns. Now it grants constant awareness of good-aligned creatures within one hundred paces — the wearer can sense their exact location and gauge their strength. The mental protections have inverted: the helm now makes it easier for the wearer to possess and dominate the weak-willed, while shielding the wearer's own consciousness during such violations. Aponavisius has claimed this for her personal armory. She says it will be useful when she takes Kenabres.

### Item the Eleventh: Mercy's Reach → Torment's Grasp

*Completed 17th of Rova, 4675 AR*

**Provenance.** A glaive wielded by the battlefield healers of the Order of the Sunrise. The original design allowed priest-warriors to reach wounded soldiers even in the thick of combat.

**Before.** The weapon's enchantment allowed it to channel healing energy at range — with a touch of the blade's tip, the wielder could mend grievous wounds even at the full extension of its reach. Three times daily it could release a burst of healing that would restore even those near death. It saved countless lives at the Battle of Keeper's Canyon.

**After.** The healing has become harming. Now it channels waves of excruciating pain at range that leave victims writhing and incapacitated, and its burst has become an explosion of agony that can stun entire groups. Particularly effective because former allies remember it as a healing weapon and approach willingly. The psychological damage when it wounds rather than heals is... substantial. Currently being studied by our torturers for new techniques.

### Item the Twelfth: Absolver's Pendant → Corruptor's Medallion

*Completed 4th of Lamashan, 4675 AR*

**Provenance.** An amulet blessed by High Priestess Serentha of Kenabres. Given to crusaders undertaking penance journeys into the Worldwound to seek redemption for failures in battle.

**Before.** A silver medallion bearing the scales of justice and the sword of valor. It granted divine protection against evil magic, and once per week the wearer could perform the rite of atonement, absolving sins and lifting curses from the repentant. Many crusaders credited it with saving their souls as well as their lives.

**After.** The silver has tarnished to black and the holy symbols have warped into blasphemous sigils. Now it protects against good-aligned magic. The weekly ritual is inverted — it can now perform an unhallowing, consecrating ground to evil powers and infusing areas with negative energy that persists for months. Several sites in the Worldwound have been so marked. The land itself begins to hunger. I wear this one myself. Joran asked me why. I told him it reminds me of what I have become. He did not ask again.

### Observations and Notes

The process becomes easier with each working. The first corruptions required three days each and left Joran physically ill. Now he can complete the work in two days, sometimes less. The forge has learned what he requires of it, or perhaps he has learned its language. I merely observe, and ensure his commitment does not waver.

My brother grows increasingly reluctant in his duties. He works the forge because I command it, but he will not look at the items afterward. Last week I found him weeping in the chapel ruins. When I asked why, he said he could still hear them screaming — all of them, every item he has corrupted, crying out in the voices of their former wielders. I hear nothing but the forge's satisfied crackling. I reminded him that his skills serve a greater purpose now. He did not argue. He never does.

### The Matter of the Sword of Valor

I have petitioned Lady Aponavisius repeatedly regarding the Sword of Valor. That battle standard, emblazoned with Iomedae's holy symbol of sun and sword, has stood as a symbol of crusader hope for decades. To corrupt it would be to break something fundamental in the crusader psyche. But she will hear no more of it. She cannot hang the artifact — doing so would activate its wards and make the citadel inhospitable to her kind. So instead she has cast it into the dungeons beneath Drezen, where her xacarba servant coils around it in the darkness.

> "Let it rot in the deep," she said. "The crusaders will never reclaim it, and I will not risk awakening its power. Some trophies are best kept where they cannot bite."

I think she is a fool to let such potential go to waste. But I have learned not to press the matter.

*— S.V.*

### Addendum

*12th of Lamashan, 4675 AR — added later, in a different ink*

General Khorramzadeh reports that Breaker of Wills performed exceptionally at the Fourth Shield Wall. Three hundred crusaders broke and fled. Among them was Captain Marlise Rhestine, daughter of the blade's last righteous wielder. She recognized it. They say she has been mute since. Some victories are more complete than others.

*— S.V.*
