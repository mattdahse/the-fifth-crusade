# The Riftwardens' Children

*What the company has pieced together of Rabiah's mark and the hunt behind it — from her mythic dream, from Cornelia Dewfoot's revelation, from the Plagued One's grim ledger, and from Arueshalae's warning.*

### The Spiral and the Plan

Rabiah has carried the mark since infancy: a spiral upon her belly that her foster mother bade her hide from the world — the Seeker's Spiral, the sigil of the Riftwardens, that secret order which keeps watch over the thin places where worlds touch and sees that nothing crosses which should not. Her true parents bore it, or gave it to her; they went into the Worldwound on some errand of the order and never came back. What the dream at the Gray Garrison began to tell her, the seasons since have made plainer.

The mark was never hers alone. The Riftwardens had made of it a design — the Riftwalker plan, they called it: cohorts of marked children, gathered and raised in the hope that, if they could all be brought to maturity together, their joined and destined power might do what armies could not, and seal the Worldwound at last. Rabiah is, so far as anyone knows, the sole survivor of the second such cohort. She was not the first.

### The One Who Came Before

**Cornelia Dewfoot** — the too-watchful halfling who attached herself to Rabiah's camp behind a mask of simple devotion — bears the same spiral. She is the lone survivor of the first cohort, born some twenty years before Rabiah, and her wary shadowing of the young sorcerer was never suspicion but recognition: one of the destined knowing another of her kind, come round again a generation later. Two survivors of two cohorts, a score of years apart, and between them the whole failed hope of the order that made them.

### The Plagued One and Her Ledger

They were hunted. The children of the spiral had enemies on both sides of the Wound, and the deadliest of these was the **Plagued One** — a Worm-That-Walks, a thing of massed vermin wearing the shape of a woman — who raised the **Red Swarm** for a single purpose: to run the destined survivors to ground and end them, one by one. When at last the company brought her down, they found among her keepings a book bound in human skin, and sewn into its spine, page upon page, were birthmarks — spiral after nearly-identical spiral, a tally of the marked children she had hunted and taken. A whole legacy, catalogued in flesh.

### The Fading Pages

The book has begun to change. Its pages are losing their color, the sewn spirals paling one by one — and as they fade, something in Rabiah quickens. She has grown ever more attuned to the shifting of the planes. She feels it now when a demon folds space to flee her presence — the tug and snap of a teleport, as plain to her as a draft through an opened door. More than feel it: she has begun to believe that, were she to steel and ready herself for it, she might shut that door before a fleeing fiend could step through it, or keep one from tearing the air open to call its allies to its side. Whatever power the order hoped to gather in all its children, some portion of it seems to be gathering, late and strange, in the last of them.

### Arueshalae's Warning

It was **Arueshalae**, the penitent, who set the last piece in place. She had learned in her long flight through the Worldwound that the Riftwalker plan had failed — the cohorts broken, the children scattered and slain — but that the last survivors of the destined were being hunted even now. And she carried a warning meant for Rabiah above all others: that **Deskari**'s forces want her dead, and will not cease to want it.

The spiral, **Iomedae** told her in the dream, always completes its journey, though rarely along the path we expect. What journey remains for the last daughter of the spiral, no one yet knows. But she no longer hides the mark.
