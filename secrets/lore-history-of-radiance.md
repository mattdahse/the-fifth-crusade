# The History of Radiance

*The remembered history of the intelligent longsword ***Radiance*** — its forging, its fallen champions, and the guardian that woke for **Harlock**.*

### Origins

Radiance was forged in the early days of Iomedae's ascension to godhood, crafted by master smiths of Lastwall who imbued it with divine blessing. Originally intended as a ceremonial blade for the newly established Order of Iomedae, the sword soon revealed its true purpose during the first demonic incursions that would eventually form the Worldwound.

### Early Wielders

The sword's first champion was Ser Darien, a founding member of the Eagle Watch. Though successful in many campaigns, Darien ultimately fell defending a village on the edge of the growing corruption. As he died, something unusual occurred—a fragment of his consciousness, his protective nature and sense of duty, became bound to the blade.

### The Guardian Emerges

With each subsequent wielder, Radiance absorbed not just their experiences but also fragments of their spirits—especially those who died in battle. Most prominent among these was Lady Celenae, the sword's second significant bearer, who sacrificed herself to save a group of refugees by holding a narrow pass alone against overwhelming odds.

### Yaniel's Legacy

When Radiance came to Yaniel, the sword already harbored the composite essence of its fallen bearers. Yaniel's strong personality and conviction resonated powerfully with the blade. Their partnership was legendary—until their fateful disagreement with the crusade leadership.

Believing she had located a crucial weakness in the Worldwound's defenses, Yaniel embarked on an unauthorized mission deep into corrupted territory. The sword, having "witnessed" similar brave but doomed endeavors before, tried to dissuade her but ultimately could not prevent her determination.

When Yaniel fell, her death struck the collective consciousness within Radiance particularly hard. Her final moments—the realization that she had been lured into a trap, her desperate last stand, and her regret at not heeding the sword's warnings—became the dominant memory within the blade.

### The Sleeping Guardian

Following Yaniel's death, Radiance was recovered but fell dormant, seemingly refusing new wielders. In truth, the collective consciousness was in mourning, reluctant to form another bond only to experience another loss. The sword was eventually placed among the relics of Kenabres, waiting for a champion who might reawaken it.

### Harlock's Discovery

When Harlock discovered Radiance, the sword sensed something different—perhaps his unconventional path to paladinhood or his half-orc heritage marking him as an unlikely champion. This uniqueness intrigued the dormant consciousness enough to form a tentative connection.

The events at the Gray Garrison and Harlock's awakening as a mythic hero have fully roused the guardian within the blade. Now Radiance speaks again, carrying the collective wisdom and trauma of its past bearers—particularly Yaniel's regret and unfinished mission—creating a guardian determined not to lose another champion to the Worldwound.

The sword now faces its own test of faith: Can it overcome its fear of failure to truly embrace its new partnership with Harlock? Or will its overprotective nature potentially hold back the very champion it wishes to preserve?
